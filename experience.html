Polytechnic Forum 2023


❮ ❯

Held from 15-22 September last year, Polytechnic Forum 2023 was a week's long programme where I have learnt many things from the journey about creating a resilient future for Singapore, which was part of the overall Poly Forum theme, 'Our Green SG', whereby we had multiple learning journeys in both the local and overseas components.

Those learning journeys in the local component took place at Gardens by the Bay (Urban Wetland Tour), where I gained insights into Singapore's 'City in a Garden' vision, and understood the overall roles of various flora and fauna, such as mangroves, which slows down water flow and filter debris, as well as pollutants. On top of that, my group also visited the Edible Garden City, where I learnt about the importance of growing our own crops, and Singapore's ambitious 30 by 30 plan.

In the overseas component, the learning journey visit to the Desaru Fruit Farm in Malaysia, allowed for the exploration of the inherent economy and bio-farming systems. Additionally, we went on a mangrove tour, which emphasised the vital role of mangroves in safeguarding shorelines, supporting diverse wildlife, and preserving the health of the ocean.

This 6 days programme was indeed an eye-opening experience, and the activities that I've been through during the Poly Forum has taught me a lot on the importance of creating a resilient future for Singapore, and all of the things that we learnt teach us that we have to take further actions ahead of time to combat the challenges ahead.

Minister Engagements


With a keen interest in politics, attending various minister engagements has provided a valuable platform for me to interact with political office holders and gain insight into the various policies and initiatives implemented by the government, as well as key issues faced in Singapore.

Among the numerous engagements I have attended, two stood out: the Youths' Future Dialogue 2023 with then Deputy Prime Minister Lawrence Wong which was held last year, and the Dialogue with Prime Minister Lawrence Wong, held in July this year.

Youths' Future Dialogue 2023, was a session where youths from various educational institutes came together to discuss and share our views on 4 main theme - Lifes & Dreams, Environment, Social and Economy. I was assigned the theme Environment, where views on the impact of environmental efforts in Singapore, as well as the topic on Singapore's Green Plan 2030 were shared and discussed, before being consolidated into a short presentation to share with then DPM Wong. This was followed by a dialogue with then DPM Wong, where questions were raised, and valuable insights were shared on how we, as youths can play a part in shaping Singapore's future.

The Dialogue with PM Lawrence Wong held in July, was a session where youths of various educational institutes came together to share our views and raised questions on potential issues that we thought were revelant to today's society. Through the dialogue, I gained a deeper understanding on the various topics and questions shared by different youths, including questions on the workforce "How can we balance between the benefits and the downfalls of a larger foreign workforce and protect Singaporean's interest" and other interesting questions on life lesson, such as "In view of the developments that affects our lives today, such as AI and the Covid-19 pandemic, if you could go back in time to 2011 and give the then newly elected Lawrence Wong a piece of advice, what would it be" These questions posed, paired with the response of PM Wong, provided valuable insights to key lessons learnt and challenges that may lie ahead.
One of my biggest takeaway from this session was, the quote by PM Wong, "start from within. Do not compare, do not ask what your friends are doing. We all have our own abilities and strengths, there is no need to compare with others to squeeze into boxes we were not meant to fit. Instead, follow your own talents and strive to reach your own full potential." This resonated with me, reminding me to focus on and pursue what I truly enjoy and am passionate about, instead of chasing after society's definition of 'perfect' and 'ideal', in fear of not fitting in.
